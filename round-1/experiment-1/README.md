## Full Summary

This experiment implements and compares two near-duplicate text detection methods on the Quora Question Pairs (QQP) dataset.

**Dataset**: 10,000 balanced pairs (5,000 duplicates, 5,000 non-duplicates) sampled from QQP (nyu-mll/glue), split 60/20/20 into train/val/test.

**Methods**:
- **Baseline — MinHash (128 hash functions, 3-gram shingling)**: Standard locality-sensitive hashing approach. Jaccard similarity estimated from signature agreement.
- **Proposed — Circular Text Fingerprints (CTF, R∈{1,2,3})**: Adapts ECFP molecular fingerprints to token sequences. Each token accumulates neighborhood hashes across R iterations; the resulting feature set is compared via exact Jaccard similarity.

**Results** (test set, n=2000, thresholds optimized on train+val):
| Method    | R   | Overall F1 | Short F1 | Medium F1 | Long F1 |
|-----------|-----|-----------|----------|-----------|---------|
| MinHash   | N/A | 0.6667    | 0.5000   | 0.7342    | 0.6050  |
| CTF       | 1   | 0.7551    | 0.5000   | 0.7591    | 0.7535  |
| CTF       | 2   | 0.7547    | 0.5000   | 0.7542    | 0.7580  |
| CTF       | 3   | 0.7555    | 0.5000   | 0.7648    | 0.7479  |

**Outcome**: SUCCESS — CTF (R=3) achieves F1=0.7555 vs MinHash F1=0.6667, a **+0.0888 improvement** (well above the 0.03 threshold). The improvement is strongest on medium/long texts. Short texts (<40 chars) remain tied at F1=0.50, suggesting token-level neighborhood propagation provides minimal additional signal when questions are very short (few tokens to propagate context across).

**Key finding**: Iterative neighborhood hashing adapted from molecular fingerprints transfers meaningfully to text near-duplicate detection. The CTF approach captures structural token context that 3-gram shingling misses, yielding consistent gains across medium and long text lengths. The method is deterministic, parameter-free (no training), and runs in O(n·R·window) time per pair.
